# Executive Summary

## Project Title

Customer Segmentation and Churn Analytics in European Banking

## Objective

The objective of this project is to analyze customer churn patterns in a European banking dataset and identify high-risk customer segments using customer segmentation techniques and data analytics.

## Problem Statement

Customer churn leads to reduced customer lifetime value, increased acquisition costs, and revenue instability. Banks often track churn rates but lack detailed segmentation insights needed to identify which customer groups are more likely to leave.

## Methodology

The project follows a structured analytical workflow:

1. Data Cleaning and Validation
2. Feature Engineering
3. Customer Segmentation
4. Exploratory Data Analysis
5. Churn Pattern Identification
6. Logistic Regression Modeling
7. Interactive Dashboard Development

## Key Findings

* Overall churn rate: 20.37%
* Germany shows higher churn compared to France and Spain.
* Customers aged 46–60 exhibit higher churn rates.
* Inactive customers churn significantly more than active customers.
* High-balance customers contribute considerable revenue risk when they churn.

An interactive Streamlit dashboard was developed to provide decision-makers with customer segmentation insights and churn analytics for better retention strategies.
